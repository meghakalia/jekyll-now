Biography

I am Megha Kalia, a PhD candidate at University of British Columbia (UBC), Vancouver, Canada, who is working towards an ambitious goal of building an Augmented Reality/ Mixed Reality (AR/MR) system for Robot Assisted Surgery (RAS) and test it on human patients within the duration of my PhD.

I work at Robotics and Control Lab, Vancouver and am supervised by Prof. Tim Salcudean, from UBC and Prof Nassir Navab from Technical University of Munich, Germany. I research on the topics of computer vision, perception in robotics, human computer interaction and human perception inspired AR/MR. Out of all these topics human perception inspired AR/MR with the application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is closest to my heart.

I received my Master of Technology (M.Tech) from Indian Institute of Technology (India), Kharagpur in Medical Imaging and Informatics. During my master I got DAAD scholarship to carry out my thesis for 7 months at TeAAAAAA

I am fortunate to have a fantastic interdisciplinary team in Robotics and Control Lab, Vancouver General Hospital (VGH) and Technical University of Munich (TUM), Germany, working together towards making AR/MR for RAS a reality. What makes us stand-out is our decades long excellent collaboration with Dr Peter Black, a Urologist at VGH, who has tested many of our techniques during robotic prostate surgeries at VGH. Such past paced execution of methods from inception to implementation in surgery has kept me hooked over the years.

Thanks to our great interdisciplinary collaborations, I have won awards like Public Scholars Initiative and has also got one Outstanding Paper Award.

When I am not working you can find me either running, biking, or reading some Tolstoy masterpiece.
